Obviously, of course

12 March 2015

Imposter syndrome is a psychological phenomenon in which people are unable to internalize their accomplishments. Wikipedia

Many successful, ambitious people feel like this; afraid they don't belong with their peers. At times, I've experienced this myself. Presenting in a room full of brilliant engineers and product experts, all silently listening to what I have to say; and suddenly feel like I'm not qualified enough to say anything of value.

I've also started noticing when others experience imposter syndrome.

What I've realized is, that when I see this happening to someonelse, my reaction can quickly turn someone who is nervous and unsure of themself into a confident speaker. Simply by how I respond and engage with them.

Take for example your word choice. I often catch myself midsentence, having said "obviously" and instantly feeling like I've insulted the person I'm talking to. Which of course isn't what I'm trying to do.

Just think about the word obviously.

obviously - in a way that is easily perceived or understood; clearly.

It undermines the intelligence of the person you're talking to, and can reinforce those who have imposter syndrome.

Because of this I've begun trying to say of course instead. Along with many other word substitutions.

At the same time, I've also began realizing that just because someone says something in a way that makes me feel like an idiot for not knowing, doesn't necessarily mean they think I'm an idiot.

They might very well think I'm an idiot; but I'd like to think they're also beating themselves up mid-sentence for having said obviously instead of of course.

Chris Demeke
